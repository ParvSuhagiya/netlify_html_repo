Jaipur

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is popularly known as the “Pink City” because of the distinct pink-colored buildings in its old town. Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, the city is famous for its planned layout and magnificent architecture. Jaipur is a place where history, culture, and traditions blend beautifully, attracting tourists from across the globe.

The city is dotted with grand palaces, forts, and temples such as the Hawa Mahal, City Palace, Amber Fort, and Jantar Mantar, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Its vibrant bazaars are equally famous, offering traditional jewelry, handicrafts, textiles, and colorful Rajasthani attire. The unique architecture and artistry of Jaipur reflect the royal heritage of Rajasthan.

Beyond monuments and markets, Jaipur is also known for its warm hospitality and rich cultural festivals. Events like the Jaipur Literature Festival and Teej bring together art, literature, music, and traditions in a lively manner. With its blend of royal charm and modern growth, Jaipur stands as a city that captures the essence of Rajasthan’s history and culture.

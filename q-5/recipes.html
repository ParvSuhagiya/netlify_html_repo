Masala Dosa

Ingredients

    For Dosa Batter
  • 2 cups rice
  • ½ cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 2 tbsp chana dal (optional, for crispiness)
  • ½ tsp fenugreek seeds (methi)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed)
      • For Potato Masala Filling
    • 4 medium potatoes (boiled & mashed)
    • 1 large onion (thinly sliced)
    • 2 green chilies (chopped)
    • 1-inch ginger (grated)
    • 1 tsp mustard seeds
    • 1 tsp turmeric powder
    • 8–10 curry leaves
    • 2 tbsp oil
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)

Recipe

  1. Prepare the Batter: Wash and soak rice, urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds for 6–8 hours. Grind to a smooth paste, add water if needed, and ferment overnight until it rises. Add salt before making dosas.
  2. Make Potato Masala: Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, curry leaves, green chilies, and ginger. Sauté onions until soft. Add turmeric and mashed potatoes, mix well, and season with salt. Garnish with coriander leaves.
  3. Cook the Dosa: Heat a tawa (griddle), pour a ladle of batter, and spread into a thin circle. Drizzle a little oil around edges and cook until golden brown and crisp.
  4. Assemble: Place potato masala in the center of the dosa, fold, and serve hot with chutney and sambar.
Fermentation
Let the dosa batter ferment overnight to get a light, fluffy, and crispy texture.
Non-stick Tawa
Use a well-greased or non-stick tawa to prevent dosa from sticking.
Thin Spreading
Spread the batter thin in a circular motion for crispier dosas.

Paneer Butter Masala

Ingredients

  • 200g paneer (cubes)
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 2 onions (chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies (optional)
  • 10–12 cashews
    • Soak in warm water
    • Grind to a smooth paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 tbsp fresh cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Recipe

  1. Heat oil and butter in a pan, add cumin seeds.
  2. Sauté onions until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook well.
  4. Mix in tomato puree and cook until oil separates.
  5. Add cashew paste, red chili powder, turmeric, and salt.
  6. Simmer for 5–7 minutes.
  7. Add paneer cubes, garam masala, and fresh cream.
  8. Stir gently and cook for 2–3 minutes.
  9. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot with naan or rice.
Cashew Paste
Adding cashew paste makes the gravy creamy and rich.
Paneer Softness
Soak paneer cubes in warm water for 10 minutes before use to keep them soft.
Butter & Cream
Always add butter and cream at the end to avoid splitting and for extra flavor.

Gulab Jamun

Ingredients

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ cup milk (as needed for dough)
  • Oil or ghee (for deep frying)
  • For Sugar Syrup
    • 1½ cups sugar
    • 1½ cups water
    • 3–4 cardamom pods
    • Few saffron strands (optional)
    • 1 tsp rose water (optional)

Recipe

  1. In a bowl, mix milk powder, maida, baking powder, and ghee.
  2. Gradually add milk and knead into a soft, smooth dough.
  3. Divide the dough into small balls without cracks.
  4. Heat oil or ghee on low flame and fry balls until golden brown.
  5. In another pan, prepare sugar syrup by boiling sugar, water, and cardamom for 8–10 minutes.
  6. Add saffron and rose water, then turn off the flame.
  7. Soak the fried gulab jamuns in warm sugar syrup for at least 2 hours.
  8. Serve warm or chilled as desired.
Soft Dough
Knead the dough gently, avoid over-kneading to prevent hard jamuns.
Low Flame Frying
Always fry gulab jamun balls on low heat to cook them evenly.
Warm Syrup
Soak fried jamuns in warm (not hot) sugar syrup for best absorption.
Resting Time
Leave jamuns in syrup for at least 2 hours before serving for maximum flavor.
